The Origin

Born from a
Nickname.

A parent lovingly called their child "beanie". That nickname became the heart of a product built to help families grow together — financially and emotionally.

beanies.family is a family financial planning app built on privacy and love. It makes counting your beans fun, replaces financial anxiety with warm encouragement, and treats every family member — every bean — as someone who counts.

The brand exists at the intersection of two worlds: the serious responsibility of financial planning and the gentle, joyful chaos of family life. Our visual and verbal identity bridges these worlds with warmth, clarity, and playful confidence.

Critical Rule

Heritage Orange is the alert colour — never use Alert Red. Financial apps commonly use red for warnings, but beanies.family deliberately avoids this. Red creates anxiety. Heritage Orange delivers the same visual prominence with warmth instead of alarm. Budget overruns, missed targets, and notifications all use Heritage Orange.

Design System

The Nook UI

A comprehensive component system built from the brand's DNA. Every element uses squircle corners (24px+ radius), soft shadows only, and the five-colour palette. The system scales from desktop to mobile while maintaining warmth and clarity.

🟧 Squircle Corners
All cards, buttons, modals, and containers use 24px+ border radius. Never use sharp corners. This creates the soft, approachable feel that distinguishes beanies from typical finance apps.
🌫️ Soft Shadows Only
Shadows are always diffused and subtle: 0 8px 40px rgba(44,62,80,0.08). No hard drop shadows, no dark borders. Elevation is implied, never harsh.
🎨 Tinted Backgrounds
Icon containers use colour-tinted backgrounds at 8–20% opacity (e.g. orange-10, silk-20, slate-05) rather than solid fills. This creates depth without heaviness.
💊 Pill Tags
Status indicators, role labels, and category tags use pill-shaped elements (border-radius: 20px) in brand tints. "On Track" in orange-10, "Feb 2026" in silk-20.
📊 Progress Bars
All progress uses rounded bars (10px radius) with Heritage Orange → Terracotta gradient fills. Alternative: Sky Silk gradient for secondary goals. Always on a slate-05 track.
🎉 Celebration States
Completed goals transform into Heritage Orange → Terracotta gradient cards. Toast notifications slide in with the brand gradient and a party emoji. Celebratory, never mechanical.
📈 Chart System
Net worth charts use a Heritage Orange line with a gradient fill beneath (orange at 30% → transparent). Subtle grid lines at 4% white opacity. A glowing dot marks the current value. Time period selector (1W / 1M / 3M / 1Y / All) uses pill-style buttons with the active state in Heritage Orange.
💰 Financial Summary Cards
Three equal-width cards below the net worth chart: Monthly Income (green arrow icon), Monthly Expenses (orange arrow icon), Cash Flow (Deep Slate gradient background with white text). Each shows the amount, a comparison to last month, and a subtle directional indicator.

Key Design Decisions
Upcoming, Not Recent
The transaction panel shows upcoming scheduled payments rather than recent history. This is intentional — families benefit more from knowing what's coming than reviewing what's passed. It reduces financial anxiety by eliminating surprises.
Cash Flow as a Hero Metric
The third summary card uses a Deep Slate gradient to visually distinguish Cash Flow from Income and Expenses. A "Healthy 🌱" label and savings rate percentage makes the number feel like progress, not just arithmetic.
Time Period Selector
The net worth chart includes a pill-style time period selector (1W / 1M / 3M / 1Y / All). Active state uses Heritage Orange at 40% opacity with a soft glow. Inactive states are white at 35% opacity.

Hamburger Button Spec

Three horizontal lines inside a squircle container. The top two lines are equal width in Deep Slate at 50% opacity. The bottom line is shorter and in Heritage Orange — this is a deliberate brand signature that makes even the most utilitarian element feel like beanies.

Mobile-First Philosophy

The mobile header stays deliberately minimal — just three elements — to maximise content space and reduce cognitive load. Families check their finances in quick moments (at the grocery store, waiting for school pickup), so every pixel of screen real estate counts.

Trust Signals

Security & Privacy

Security is foundational to beanies.family — but it should feel safe, never scary. Our security UI communicates protection through quiet confidence, not alarming padlocks and warning banners.

💾
Save Indicator
Shows the filename where family data is saved (e.g. "bean_pod_greg.enc"). Uses a save/document icon in soft white at 30% opacity. Sits at the bottom of every sidebar.
🔐
Encryption Badge
Shows "AES-256 encrypted" with a padlock icon in soft green (#6EE7B7 at 30% opacity). The green ties to the positive-indicator colour used throughout the app. Reassuring, not alarming.
👁️
Privacy Mode
One-tap toggle that masks all financial figures with "•••••". Eye icon with a green dot when figures are visible. Available in the desktop header and the mobile hamburger menu Controls section.

Design Principle

Security indicators should be felt, not seen. They sit at low opacity (30%), use soft SVG icons, and blend into the sidebar. Users shouldn't actively notice them — but when they glance down, they feel a quiet reassurance. Like a seatbelt indicator on a dashboard: always there, never screaming.

Brand Integrity

Do's, Don'ts &
The Golden Rule

✓ Always Do
  • Use Heritage Orange for all alerts — never Alert Red
  • Maintain squircle corners (24px+) on all containers
  • Use soft, diffused shadows only
  • Keep the beanies together — parent and child always hold hands
  • Use visual icons in the header (flags, avatars, badges) not text
  • Show upcoming transactions, not just recent history
  • Include security indicators (save file + encryption) in every sidebar
  • Use warm, encouraging language in all notifications
  • Celebrate goal completions with gradient toasts
  • Keep the mobile header minimal: hamburger + greeting + bell only
✗ Never Do
  • Use Alert Red for any purpose — budget overruns, errors, or warnings
  • Use sharp corners or hard-edged shadows
  • Separate the parent and child beanies in the logo
  • Rotate, flip, stretch, or distort the logo
  • Use cold, corporate language ("Transaction failed", "Error 403")
  • Use text-only labels in the header instead of visual icons
  • Crowd the mobile header with controls (move them to hamburger)
  • Show security indicators at full opacity (always 30% max)
  • Add the logo on busy imagery without a solid backing shape
  • Recreate or redraw the bean characters
🫘

Never Separate.

The beanies are a family. They walk together, grow together, and always hold hands. This is the single most important visual rule of the brand.

Logo Don'ts — Never do these:

  • Rotate or flip the logo
  • Stretch or distort any dimension
  • Change the arrow's trajectory or colour
  • Separate parent and child beanies
  • Add drop shadows or effects not in the guidelines
  • Recreate or redraw the characters
  • Place on busy imagery without a solid backing
  • Reorder the Pod colours (always: Slate · Terracotta · Orange · Sky Silk)
