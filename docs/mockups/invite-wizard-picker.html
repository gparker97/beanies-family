Is this safe? +

Yes. The family data file is encrypted with a key only you and your beanies have. Google can't read what's inside — they're just storing the locked file for you.

What about the little beanies? +

If they don't have their own Google account yet, share with one of your own emails — you can sign them in on their device with that account, and they'll see the family pod. When they're ready for their own, you can set up a free, parent-supervised Gmail through Google Family Link.

What if they don't use Google? +

They'll need a free Google account to access the family pod — it lives in Google Drive. Setting one up takes about a minute at accounts.google.com. For kids, Google Family Link creates a free, parent-supervised Gmail.

What's changing in this iteration

  • New picker step (Step 0) — when you tap Invite Beanie from the Pod, the wizard now opens with a list of every beanie waiting to join. Owner + already-joined members appear dimmed with a status chip; pets are hidden entirely. Per-bean share button bypasses this step (goes straight to confirm-email like before).
  • "+ add a new beanie" tile at the end of the list — opens the existing add-member drawer in-place. Once the new bean is saved, the wizard advances directly to the confirm-email step with their info pre-filled.
  • Smarter email pre-fill — only fills when the bean has a real, shareable address. Temporary placeholders (pending-*@setup.local, *@temp.beanies.family) leave the field blank, surface a Sky-Silk warning chip, and add an inline expandable hint "What if my child doesn't have an email?".
  • FAQ button refresh — drops Caveat for Outfit-bold and updated copy: "You've got questions? We've got answers." Caveat is now reserved for Step 2's QR-code accent (the wizard's once-per-flow handwritten moment).
  • Invitee chip on Step 1 makes it visually clear who you're inviting, with a "change" link back to the picker — no need to close the modal to swap recipient.
